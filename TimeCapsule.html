TimeCapsule – Your Own Encrypted Digital Alarm Capsule

Problem Statement

In the digital age, people often want to preserve memories, messages, or secrets for future reflection. However, there is no secure, private, and user-controlled digital solution that allows individuals to "lock" their thoughts or messages until a specific future date. Without encryption or control, such content can be exposed, lost, or accessed prematurely.

Objective

TimeCapsule offers a simple, secure, and personal tool to write encrypted memories or notes, unlocking them only on a future date. It simulates the traditional concept of a time capsule using modern encryption techniques for complete confidentiality.

Key Features

  • Fernet Encryption: Uses AES-based Fernet encryption to lock messages securely.
  • Time-Locked Access: Messages can't be read before the chosen unlock date.
  • Secure Key Management: Encryption keys are auto-generated and securely stored.
  • Date Validation: Prevents invalid dates and premature access.
  • Simple CLI Interface: Terminal-based, easy-to-use tool for writing and reading capsules.
  • Persistent Storage: Saves encrypted capsules in local JSON files for long-term use.

Tech Stack

Language: Python

Libraries Used:
  • cryptography – For Fernet (AES-based) encryption & decryption
  • datetime – For future-date validation
  • os – For file handling
  • json – For storing capsule metadata
Data Storage:
  • secret.key – Stores encryption key
  • capsule_data.json – Holds encrypted capsules and related info
